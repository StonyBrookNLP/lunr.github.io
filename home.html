Welcome to the Language Understanding and Reasoning (LUNR) lab website, situated in Stony Brook University, New York. We are a passionate group of researchers motivated by the challenge of building systems that can extract, understand, and reason with information that exist in all forms of language.

Active Research Projects

Explainable Natural Language Inference

This NSF supported project focuses on developing explainable inference algorithms. Applications include question answering, and relation extraction. This is a collaborative research effort with co-investigators Prof. Peter Jansen and Prof. Mihai Surdeanu from University of Arizona.

Understanding Common-sense Knowledge About Events

  • Modeling conditional knowledge about events - Events typically alter the state of the world. This NSF supported project focuses on developing techniques for acquiring knowledge from news texts and simple narratives.
  • Modeling Schematic Event Knowledge - This DARPA funded project focuses on developing event language models that can reason about events.

Efficiency and Energy Consumption in NLP

Models are getting larger, consuming more compute, memory, and energy. Our work looks into ways for making NLP models faster, smaller, and more energy efficient where possible.

Verifying Complex Software with NLP

This NSF funded project is looking at developing semantic parsing systems that convert Network File System specification texts into formal statements.

Private Intelligence Assistance

This project focuses on delivering privacy focused NLP applications that can run on user devices.
